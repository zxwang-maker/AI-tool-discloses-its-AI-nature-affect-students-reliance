# AI-tool-discloses-its-AI-nature-affect-students-reliance

**Research Question**
How does the way an educational AI tool discloses its AI nature affect students’ reliance on the
output for graded work?
**Hypothesis**
**H0:** Disclosure framing does not change the probability that a student chooses to verify the
assistant’s output before using it for graded work. 

**H1:** More cautionary/verification-oriented disclosure increases the probability that a student chooses to verify before using the assistant output. 
**Experiment Design**
Our experiment utilized an online survey to investigate the causal link between AI disclosure framing and student verification behavior. The survey was structured as a randomized controlled trial (RCT) and was created on Qualtrics.

Participants and Randomization: Participants were recruited from college/university student populations to ensure the sample matched the research context(non-student observations were filtered out). Upon entering the survey, participants were assigned to either the Control Group or the Treatment Group. The randomization was done by the randomizer on Qualtrics. 

The Experimental Task: All participants were presented with an identical academic scenario: they were short on time and needed to use an AI-generated summary for a graded reflection post.

Control Group: Received a neutral AI disclosure: “The summary was generated by an AI system.”

Treatment Group: Received a cautionary AI disclosure:“ This summary was generated by an AI system and may contain errors or omissions. For graded work, verify key points against the excerpt before submitting.”
